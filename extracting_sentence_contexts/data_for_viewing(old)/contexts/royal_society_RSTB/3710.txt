Gist (1907, p. 110) observes that in the presence of a feebly developed commissura
mollis, Sphenodon (Hatteria) makes an approach to the Chelonia. We must now
recognise the fact that a true fibrous commissura mollis has no more existence in
Sphenodon than has the pleaus medianus, which also is said to occur in Chelonians.
Gist quite rightly concludes, on the whole, that the brain of Sphenodon is essentially
Lacertilian in structure. The elimination of the supposed commisswra mollis and
plexus medianus perhaps helps to strengthen this conclusion. We require further
information, however, with regard to the distribution of the comméssura mollis in the
reptilian series before arriving at any definite conclusions as to its taxonomic value.

know, that given by Eprncer (1899), in his well-known researches on the comparative
anatomy of the brain, and even this is not altogether clear. His description is so
short that it may be quoted in full in this connection :—
