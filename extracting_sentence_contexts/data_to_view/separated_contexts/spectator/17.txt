I therefore recommend to you for the Subject of a Paper the following Advertisement, which about two Months ago was given into every Body's Hands, and may be seen with some Additions in the Daily Courant of August the Ninth. 'Nicholas Hart2, who slept last Year in St. Bartholomew's Hospital, intends to sleep this Year at the Cock and Bottle in Little-Britain.' Having since inquired into the Matter of Fact, I find that the above-mentioned Nicholas Hart is every Year seized with a periodical Fit of Sleeping, which begins upon the Fifth of August, and ends on the Eleventh of the same Month: That

        On the First of that Month he grew dull;
        On the Second, appeared drowsy; On the Third, fell a yawning;
        On the Fourth, began to nod;
        On the Fifth, dropped asleep;
        On the Sixth, was heard to snore;
        On the Seventh, turned himself in his Bed; On the Eighth, recovered his former Posture;
        On the Ninth fell a stretching;
