developed where it is most required, 7.e., in the neighbourhood of the muscles of the

mind, and not merely by reference to the hyponeural and apical systems which,
from the evidence provided by the study of Marthasterias glacialis, are only parts of

it undertakes the innervation of the four intervertebral muscles responsible for the
lateral and oro-aboral flexure of the arm. In the Holothuroidea it is precisely this
centre of innervation which supplies fibrillae to the circular and longitudinal
musculature of the body wall.
