The salt lies in large contorted sheets, overlaying each other like
frozen waves of muddy ice. On the eastern and southern shores, which shelve away
gradually to more distant hills, are great flats of salt mud covered with Salicornia, &c. The biological interest of this place lies in the fact that upon the steep western
shore are marked very definite terraces, showing the position of the water at different
