1, INTRODUCTION: MECHANISMS OF UPSTREAM INFLUENCE

In the well-known inviscid theory of supersonic flow, a disturbance at a point can
have an upstream influence only if it is so strongly compressive as to reduce the
local fluid speed below that of sound. But it is now many years einco Ferri (1939)
found that disturbances can have an upstream influence, through the agency of the
boundary layer, when on the inviscid theory they would have none.
